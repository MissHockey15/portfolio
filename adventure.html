Adventure

Isometrics is a method for visually representing three-dimensional objects in two dimensions in technical and engineering drawings. It is a really unique style of art that can show a three dimentional world and design. Minecraft was what gave me most of my inspiration because that is the best example of an isometric world. It is a world where everything is made of cubes so it helped me how things look in three dimentions. The first thing that came to my mind was that I wanted to try and represent a video game with an isometric world. When anylizing the Minecraft world the next video game that came to mind was the Uncharted series. This series follows a charater named Nathan Drake through his adventures to find treasure. With that in mind I knew I wanted to show the process of finding treasure.

With my theme in mind I began sketching anything that I thought related to the theme. I planned out how I wanted my area to look first and mapped out what each layer would represent. When it comes to adventure and treasure hunting the first thing that came to mind caves. I used caves as my starting point and started from the bottom up of my isometric world. I began sketching out everything I could think of that would be in caves like ghosts, treasure, torches, jewels, bones, etc. Once I was done that tear I move up to surface and began making a forest. Once the forest was complete I began on the next tear and combined the water route and dirt road route into one so that the piece wouldn't get too tall. Finally it was toped off with a pirate ship to give it a dynamic topper.
